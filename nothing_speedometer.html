This Privacy Policy describes how Apex Creators ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use the Nothing Speedometer application (the "App"). The App is a free, ad-supported GPS speedometer available on Android.

By downloading, installing, or using the App, you agree to the collection and use of your information as described in this Privacy Policy. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about our data practices. We will not use or share your information with anyone except as described in this Privacy Policy.

1. Information Collection and Use

For a better experience while using our Service, we may require access to certain device data, including Device Identifiers (Advertising ID) and usage data. Data requested locally is retained on your device and is not collected by us directly, except for data collected by third-party service providers as described below.

2. Location Data

Our App requires access to your device's location to function as a speedometer.

  • Fine and Coarse Location: We collect location data to calculate your current speed, total distance traveled, and average speed.
  • Usage: This data is processed locally on your device in real-time. We do not transmit your location history to any external servers, nor do we store it for any purpose other than displaying your current trip statistics within the App.

3. Analytics Data (Firebase Analytics)

To improve the performance, reliability, and user experience of the App, we use Google Analytics for Firebase. Firebase collects certain information automatically, including:

  • App Activity Events: Standard usage data such as opening the app, viewing specific screens, applying a watch face, starting/stopping a trip, toggling HUD mode, or adjusting setting preferences.
  • Device Information: Device model, operating system, and system configuration settings.
  • General Location Data: Derived geographic location metadata (such as country or region level). We do not send your precise GPS coordinates or real-time location tracking data to Firebase.
  • Device/Advertising Identifiers: User-resettable identifiers such as the Android Advertising ID (AAID) for analytics and attribution purposes.

This data is stored on Google's secure servers and is analyzed in aggregate to monitor app performance, debug crash/load issues, and understand user engagement. No personally identifiable information (PII) is linked to this analytics data.

4. Advertising – Google AdMob

The App displays advertisements served by Google AdMob, a mobile advertising platform operated by Google LLC. In compliance with Google's Publisher Policies (updated June 2026), we disclose the following about how AdMob collects and uses your data.

4.1 Data Collected Automatically by the Google Mobile Ads SDK

The Google Mobile Ads SDK (GMA SDK) automatically collects and shares the following data for the purposes of advertising, analytics, and fraud prevention:

  • IP Address: Used to estimate your approximate geographic location for ad relevance.
  • Android Advertising ID (GAID): A unique, user-resettable device identifier used for interest-based ad targeting and analytics.
  • User Interactions: App launch events, ad impressions, and tap interactions used to measure ad performance and user engagement.
  • Diagnostic Data: Non-personal crash logs and SDK performance metrics used to improve ad delivery stability.

All data collected by the Google Mobile Ads SDK is encrypted in transit using TLS (Transport Layer Security).

4.2 Personalized vs. Non-Personalized Ads

AdMob may serve personalized (interest-based) ads using your Advertising ID. You may opt out of personalized advertising at any time through your Android device settings:

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization"

4.3 Advertising ID Controls

You may reset or delete your Advertising ID at any time through your device's ad settings. On Android 13 and above, deleting the Advertising ID replaces it with a string of zeros, after which no personalized ads will be served.

4.4 EU / EEA, UK & Switzerland – GDPR Consent

GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the App will display a consent dialog before serving personalized ads. This is required under the EU ePrivacy Directive and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) as per Google's EU User Consent Policy. You may withdraw your consent at any time through the App's settings or your device's ad controls.

4.5 US State Privacy Laws

CCPA / CPRA

For users in US states with applicable privacy laws (including California CPRA, Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, and others), Google AdMob supports Restricted Data Processing (RDP) and honors the Global Privacy Platform (GPP) signal. Where required by law, we limit data processing and honor opt-out requests for the sale or sharing of personal data for targeted advertising.

4.6 Children and Advertising

We do not knowingly serve personalized ads to children under 13. Where required, we apply Google's child-directed treatment tags (TFCD / TFUA) to ensure that the Advertising ID and other identifiers are not transmitted for child-directed ad requests. See also Section 9 (Children's Privacy).

4.7 Google's Privacy Policy & AdMob Resources

Data collected via AdMob is governed by Google's own Privacy Policy. For full details:

  • AdMob Help Center – How AdMob uses data

  • The right to rectification – You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate.
  • The right to erasure – You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to restrict processing – You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to object to processing – You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

7. CCPA Privacy Rights (Do Not Sell My Personal Information)

Under the CCPA, among other rights, California consumers have the right to:

  • Request that a business that collects a consumer's personal data disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal data that a business has collected about consumers.
  • Request that a business delete any personal data about the consumer that a business has collected.
  • Request that a business that sells a consumer's personal data, not sell the consumer's personal data.

If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us.

8. Data Deletion & Retention

We do not store your personal location data on external servers. All trip data and settings are stored locally on your device.

  • Deleting App Data: You can delete all data stored by the App by clearing the app data in your device settings or by uninstalling the application. Uninstalling the app permanently removes all local history and settings.
  • Third-Party Data: For data collected by Firebase Analytics, AdMob, or Google Play Services, please refer to their respective privacy policies linked above for information on data deletion, management, and retention periods.

9. Children's Privacy

These Services do not address anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. Regarding advertising, we do not knowingly serve personalized ads to children and apply appropriate child-directed treatment flags (TFCD / TFUA) where applicable. In the case we discover that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we immediately delete this from our servers. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so that we will be able to take the necessary actions.
